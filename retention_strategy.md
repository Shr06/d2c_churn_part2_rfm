# Retention Strategy Report

## Segment 1: Champions

### Behaviour

* 401 customers
* Highest average spend (₹2,426.32)
* Highest purchase frequency (3.14 orders)
* Most recent purchasing activity (20.47 days)
* Strong digital engagement (7.49 sessions)

### Retention Action

* VIP loyalty program
* Early access to new products
* Personalized recommendations
* Exclusive rewards

### Expected Business Value

Champions are the company's most valuable customers and generate substantial revenue. Retaining this segment protects current revenue streams and increases customer lifetime value.

## Segment 7: Dormant Customers

### Behaviour

* 913 customers
* Largest segment
* Low average spend (₹524.18)
* Longest inactivity period (139.62 days)

* Email reminders

### Expected Business Value

# Campaign Budget Prioritization

### Assumed Retention Budget

A hypothetical retention budget of ₹100,000 is assumed because no overall campaign budget is provided in the dataset.

### Priority Order

1. High Value But Unhappy
2. At Risk
3. Loyal Customers
4. New Customers
5. Champions
6. Discount Sensitive
7. Dormant Customers

### Prioritization Logic

1.The highest priority should be High Value But Unhappy customers because they generate significant revenue while exhibiting dissatisfaction signals such as elevated return rates and support interactions.

2.The second priority should be At Risk customers because they show declining engagement and can still be recovered before becoming fully inactive.

3.Loyal Customers and New Customers receive medium priority because retaining these groups supports long-term revenue growth and customer lifetime value.

4.Champions should continue receiving loyalty benefits but require less intervention because they already demonstrate strong engagement and purchasing behaviour.

5.Discount Sensitive and Dormant Customers receive the lowest priority because their revenue contribution is comparatively lower and retention campaigns for these groups should remain cost-efficient.
