# Retention Strategy Report

## Segment 1: Champions

### Behaviour
- 401 customers
- Highest average spend (₹2,426)
- Highest purchase frequency (3.14 orders)
- Most recent purchasing activity (20 days)

### Retention Action
- VIP loyalty program
- Early access to new products
- Personalized recommendations
- Exclusive rewards

### Expected Business Value
Champions are the company's most valuable customers and generate substantial revenue. Retaining this segment protects current revenue streams and increases customer lifetime value.

---

## Segment 2: Loyal Customers

### Behaviour
- 536 customers
- Strong average spend (₹1,762)
- Frequent purchasers (2.46 orders)
- Moderate recency (77 days)

### Retention Action
- Cross-sell campaigns
- Product bundle offers
- Loyalty point incentives

### Expected Business Value
This segment represents customers with stable purchasing behaviour and strong revenue contribution. Increasing purchase frequency can significantly improve revenue.

---

## Segment 3: New Customers

### Behaviour
- 312 customers
- Very recent purchases (14 days)
- Low purchase frequency (1 order)

### Retention Action
- Welcome journey
- First-to-second purchase incentives
- Product education emails

### Expected Business Value
Converting first-time buyers into repeat customers increases long-term retention and customer lifetime value.

---

## Segment 4: At Risk

### Behaviour
- 175 customers
- Poor recency (132 days)
- Low purchase frequency (1.11 orders)
- Moderate spend

### Retention Action
- Win-back campaigns
- Personalized offers
- Reminder emails highlighting past purchases

### Expected Business Value
These customers previously engaged with the business but show declining activity. Re-engagement can recover revenue before complete churn.

---

## Segment 5: High Value But Unhappy

### Behaviour
- 61 customers
- Very high spend (₹2,126)
- Lower engagement compared to Champions
- Elevated support interaction levels

### Retention Action
- Dedicated customer support follow-up
- Personalized outreach
- Service recovery initiatives

### Expected Business Value
Although small in size, this segment contributes substantial revenue. Preventing churn among these customers provides a high return on retention investment.

---

## Segment 6: Discount Sensitive

### Behaviour
- 37 customers
- Low spend (₹424)
- High dependence on discounts

### Retention Action
- Targeted promotional campaigns
- Limited-time discounts
- Coupon-based engagement

### Expected Business Value
This segment responds strongly to pricing incentives and can be activated at relatively low cost.

---

## Segment 7: Dormant Customers

### Behaviour
- 878 customers
- Largest segment
- Lowest spending levels
- Longest inactivity period (142 days)

### Retention Action
- Low-cost reactivation campaigns
- Email reminders
- Seasonal offers

### Expected Business Value
Individual customer value is low, so retention spending should remain limited. Focus on scalable campaigns rather than personalized outreach.

---

# Campaign Budget Prioritization

Assumed Retention Budget: ₹100,000

| Priority | Segment | Budget Allocation |
|-----------|----------|------------------|
| 1 | High Value But Unhappy | ₹35,000 |
| 2 | At Risk | ₹25,000 |
| 3 | Loyal Customers | ₹15,000 |
| 4 | New Customers | ₹10,000 |
| 5 | Champions | ₹10,000 |
| 6 | Discount Sensitive | ₹3,000 |
| 7 | Dormant Customers | ₹2,000 |

## Prioritization Logic

The highest priority should be High Value But Unhappy customers because they generate significant revenue and are at elevated churn risk.

The second priority should be At Risk customers because they show declining engagement and can still be recovered before becoming fully inactive.

Champions should receive retention rewards but require less intervention because they already demonstrate strong engagement.

Dormant Customers receive the smallest allocation because their revenue contribution and likelihood of reactivation are comparatively low.
